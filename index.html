Pranav Misra

I am a Ph.D. biophysicist from Harvard University. My research sits at the intersection of cognitive neuroscience and machine learning, using large-scale intracranial recordings and modern AI models to study how language is represented in the human brain.

Broadly, I’m interested in robust and multimodal neural representations, brain–machine interfaces for language, and how ideas from linguistics and deep learning can be combined to understand human cognition.

Research

My Ph.D. work involved building and analyzing large-scale intracranial datasets (5,000+ electrodes across dozens of patients, 100+ hours of linguistic stimuli) spanning phrases, sentences, and naturalistic text. I develop models that link these recordings with language and vision–language models to study syntactic and semantic representations, and to enable more data-efficient neural decoding.

I also collaborate with clinical teams at the Cleveland Clinic and Boston Children’s Hospital to identify neural signatures of sentence processing that can inform surgical planning in epilepsy patients.
