Pranav Misra, Ph.D.

Ph.D. researcher studying multimodal representations of language in the human brain using invasive recordings and modern AI models.

About

I am Pranav Misra, from Lucknow, India, and Cambridge, Massachusetts—two places that have shaped my worldview through community, conversation, and intellectual curiosity. My academic journey bridges computer science, neuroscience, linguistic theory, and machine learning.

Starting at BITS Pilani, I explored computation through Logic, Theory of Computation, and Algorithm Design, later branching into gravitational theory and advanced calculus before returning to neural computation. Research in augmented reality and visual segmentation eventually led me to Harvard, where I studied human episodic memory during an undergraduate thesis project.

During my Ph.D. at Harvard, my work focused on multimodal intracranial signals for language—providing rigorous neuroscientific evidence for parts-of-speech, syntax, and semantic processing in the human brain [oai_citation:2‡PranavMisraCV.pdf](sediment://file_000000004abc71f5a16994bfee519036). I integrate computational linguistics, sEEG signals, deep learning models, and vision-language systems to understand how the brain forms linguistic and conceptual structure.

My broader research interest is understanding intelligent computation through neurocognitive frameworks and theory-driven modeling. I aim to explore hierarchical computation, compositionality, and biologically grounded mechanisms in neural and language models.

Research

Ph.D. in Biophysics — Harvard University (2019–2025)

Thesis: Robust and Multimodal Intracranial Signals for Language in the Human Brain [oai_citation:3‡PranavMisraCV.pdf](sediment://file_000000004abc71f5a16994bfee519036)

  • Designed and collected invasive neural datasets from 5,064 electrodes across 47 human patients with over 100+ hours of linguistic stimuli.
  • Produced novel neuroscientific evidence for linguistic theories on parts-of-speech, syntax, and semantics using multimodal recordings.
  • Applied language models to improve decoding accuracy and interpretability for brain–machine interfaces.
  • Developed cross-modal decoding pipelines integrating sentence → image and image → sentence tasks.
  • Used few-shot & semi-supervised learning for data-efficient interpretability across subjects.

Clinical Collaboration Lead — Cleveland Clinic & Boston Children’s Hospital

  • Identified neural signatures of multimodal sentence processing relevant for epilepsy surgery planning. [oai_citation:4‡PranavMisraCV.pdf](sediment://file_000000004abc71f5a16994bfee519036)
  • Accelerated a multi-institutional invasive recording study from 3 years to 6 months via cross-team coordination.

Teaching — Neuro140: Biological & Artificial Intelligence

  • Mentored 60+ students on machine learning for biomedical and neural datasets.
  • Guided projects using autoencoders, semi-supervised learning, and multimodal pipelines.
